# VIDEO BACKGROUND INSTRUCTIONS

## What You Need

You need to add a video file named `ocean-video.mp4` to make the hero section background work properly.

## Quick Steps

1. **Download a free ocean/underwater video** from one of these sites:
   - Pexels: https://www.pexels.com/videos/search/ocean/
   - Pixabay: https://pixabay.com/videos/search/underwater/
   - Videvo: https://www.videvo.net/free-videos/ocean

2. **Search for videos like:**
   - "ocean waves"
   - "underwater blue"
   - "fish swimming"
   - "bluefin tuna"
   - "sea water"
   - "ocean surface"

3. **Download the video** (MP4 format)

4. **Rename it to:** `ocean-video.mp4`

5. **Place it in the same folder** as index.html

## Recommended Video

For best results, choose a video that shows:
- ✅ Clear blue ocean water
- ✅ Underwater scenes with fish (if possible)
- ✅ Calm, professional footage
- ✅ 10-30 seconds duration
- ✅ High quality (1920x1080 or better)

## Alternative: Use a Gradient Background

If you don't want to use a video right now, you can temporarily use a gradient background.

Open `index.html` and replace this section:

```html
<!-- Video Background -->
<div class="hero-video-container">
    <video class="hero-video" autoplay muted loop playsinline>
        <source src="ocean-video.mp4" type="video/mp4">
    </video>
    <div class="hero-overlay"></div>
</div>
```

With this:

```html
<!-- Gradient Background (temporary) -->
<div class="hero-video-container" style="background: linear-gradient(135deg, #0B3C5D 0%, #1565C0 50%, #00ACC1 100%);">
    <div class="hero-overlay"></div>
</div>
```

This will give you a beautiful ocean-blue gradient until you add the video.
